Becoming an ASME.org Participant

Are existing members required to become ASME.org Participants? Answer: No, a member does not have to become a Participant. An unconverted member or customer can go to www.asme.org and sign in with the username and password that they previously used to sign in to their MYASME Account. They will have access to the following account information: Account Information, Membership & Benefits Access, Purchase History, and Committee History.

Why should existing members become Participants? Answer: Becoming a Participant gives a member the ability to engage with ASME in a new way through our online engineering community. Participants can create Profiles, connect with other individuals with similar interests, and join and create Groups focused on engineering topics, including Groups for official ASME units. With this tool, Participants have the benefit of making their voices heard on the latest in engineering and ASME news while communicating with fellow engineers from around the world, all on one platform.

Do existing members need to become Participants to access Member Benefits? Answer: No, a user does not have to become a Participant in order to access their Member Benefits. A non-converted user will have access to their Member Benefits through an abbreviated dashboard.

Do existing members need to become Participants to participate in online ASME Groups? Answer: Yes, a member will have to become a participant in order to participate in ASME Groups. However, "public" Group content can be viewed by any user.

Do users have to become Participants in order to purchase a product? Answer: No, a user does not have to become a participant in order to purchase a product. Access to ASME products will still be available to ALL ASME users.

If I don’t want to become a Participant, where do I go to renew my membership dues or update my contact information? Answer: An unconverted member or customer can go to www.asme.org and sign in with the username and password that they previously used to sign in to their MYASME Account. They will have access to a Membership & Benefits page that will provide the option to renew their membership. The user can also update their name, email address, phone number, and mailing address.


Registration

What are the steps to complete registration for an existing member or customer with an online account? Answer:

  • Go to www.asme.org
  • On the right-hand side, enter your primary email address and password. Your primary email address and password is the information that is used when you sign into your MYASME Account.
  • Upon successful log-in, you will arrive on the Account Information Page.
  • On the left hand side, click Create Profile.
  • On the Create Profile page you must enter the following information to create a public Profile:
    • First/Last Name
    • Primary Interest
    • Country, City, State, Time Zone
  • Read and Agree to the Terms of Use (you must scroll through the entire Terms of Use section in order to proceed).
  • Click Register.
  • The Almost Done page will appear to let you know to check your email in order to validate your registration.
  • Check your email and click the email validation link. This will then take you to the sign in page. The email verification link is only active for 48 hours.
  • You should now have the ability to sign into your Participant dashboard, using the same username and password entered during the initial sign in.

What are the steps to complete registration for a New User? Answer:

  • Go to www.asme.org
  • Click Create Account at the top right of your screen.
  • You will be required to enter the following info in order to register:
    • First/Last Name
    • Email
    • Password
    • Primary Interest
    • Country, City, State, Time Zone
    • Member ID is not required
  • Read and Agree to the Terms of Use (you must scroll through the entire Terms of Use section in order to proceed).
  • Click Register.
  • The Almost Done page will appear to let you know to check your email in order to validate your registration.
  • Check your email and click the email validation link. This will then take you to the sign in page. The email verification link is only active for 48 hours.
  • You should now have the ability to sign into your Participant dashboard, using the same username and password entered during the initial sign in.

What is my primary email address with ASME?

Answer: Your primary email address is the email address used to access your MYASME account.

How can I change my primary email address?

Answer: Contact Customer Care at customercare@asme.org or 800-843-2763. They will be able to assist you in changing your primary email address.

Log-In

I registered as a Participant, but I did not receive my account activation email.

Answer:

  • Check your spam folder to ensure the email was not sent to spam
  • Upon completing registration, an Almost Done page will display. Click resend at the bottom of this page to resend the activation email.
  • If you still do not receive your activation email, or if you have already navigated away from the Almost Done page, please send an email to asmecommunity@asme.org. Include the email address you used to register in your message.
I am currently an ASME member or customer and I can't sign in with my current MYASME username and password.

Answer:

  • If the email address you're using is your primary email address from your MYASME Account, the password you're entering may be incorrect. Click "Forgot Password" located under Sign In to retrieve your password. An email will be sent to the email address you've entered.
  • If the system does not prompt you to check your email, the email address entered may not be your primary email address. If you are a member please contact Customer Care to confirm or update your primary email address at customercare@asme.org. If you are not a member, please register to become a Participant.

How do I find my Dashboard and Profile pages?

Answer: In the top right corner you will see your name. When you roll over your name, you will see a drop-down menu that will allow you to navigate to your Dashboard, Profile page, and privacy settings as well as signing out.

Whenever you sign in, you will be taken directly to your Dashboard. The Dashboard makes it easy for you to navigate to your official ASME Groups as well as manage your ASME account.

Forgot Password

I don't remember my password.

Answer: Click "Forgot Password" located under Sign In to retrieve your password. For all ASME members, you may enter the email address previously used to access your MYASME account. For all new Participants, please enter the email address used to register and click Reset Password. An email with further instructions on resetting your password will be sent.

I didn't receive the email to reset my password.

Answer: Please send an email to asmecommunity@asme.org and include your email address. We will provide you with a temporary password.

Profile

How do I make my Profile 100% complete?

Answer: To complete your Profile, please update the following information:

  • Photo
  • Job Title
  • Professional Summary
  • Work History
  • Education
  • Primary Interest
  • At least 2 Secondary Interests
We also encourage all users to join Groups, connect with colleagues, and engage in conversations through commenting and posting.

How do I enter a Certificate that doesn't expire?

Answer: To enter a Certificate that does not expire, on the Profile page under Certificates, click next to the box that states "This certificate does not expire."

Dashboard

The information on my Membership & Benefits page is not accurate.

Answer: The information on your Membership & Benefits page is linked directly to your current ASME membership. If there is inaccurate information reflected on your Membership & Benefits page, please send an email to asmecommunity@asme.org, providing us with your email address and the information that is incorrect.

How do I control my Privacy Settings?

Answer: Each user has the ability to limit the information that is viewable on their Profile page. By default most features are set to Public. A user has the ability to adjust their visible features to Public, ASME.org Participants, Your Network, or Private. To change your Privacy Settings, go to your Dashboard and on the left hand side click "Privacy Settings." Once you have updated your settings click Save Changes.

Groups

How do I find my ASME Groups?

Answer: If you have already joined Groups, the best way to access them is by looking at the Groups tab on your Dashboard. This is personalized to you.

One of the Groups to which I belong is not listed on my Dashboard — what should I do?

Answer: Go to community.asme.org/search/directorysearch.aspx and look for the Group there. Some Groups are open and can be joined by clicking on the Join Group button on the Group's landing page.

I searched for my Group but it didn't come up in the Directory — what should I do?

Answer: The Group you’re seeking may be a closed or inactive Group. Make sure that the Group Access Type is set to “All Groups” to see these as well. For Groups that are closed, please send an email to asmecommunity@asme.org with the name of the Group in which you would like to be enrolled and we will contact the Group’s administrator. Inactive Groups do not have an administrator — if you are interested in becoming the administrator for an inactive Group, please send an email to asmecommunity@asme.org.

I just signed in, why am I already a member of a Group(s)?

Answer: All members that currently hold leadership roles within Committees, Divisions, and Sections should be auto-enrolled into their respective Committee, Division, and Section Groups. For these Participants, the first item on their Site Activity tab on their Dashboard is “[Participant Name] has joined the Group [Name of Group].” Links to their Groups will be found on the Groups tab of the Dashboard.

Search

How do I find other Participants?

Answer: : Use the Directory! You can access the Participant Directory either by clicking on "Communities" above the ASME logo on every ASME.org page and selecting "ASME Community Directory" from the sidebar, or by using the search box at the top right of every ASME.org page.

I don't want my Profile to show up in Search.

Answer: All Participants by default are searchable within the Directory. Participants have the option of opting out of the Directory. To opt out of the Directory, please go to your Dashboard. Under "Manage Account" on the left hand side click "Privacy Settings." Under "Visibility Options" at the bottom of the page select "Yes" and then click "Save Changes."
